Historic Hikes

by Carl Carlson

Soda Springs, although well known for it's captive guyser, has also become a destination for hikers and history buffs. It lies on one of the oldest routes in the Northwest. Various native tribes used to travel through Soda Springs on their way to the healing waters at what is now Lava Hot Springs. Fur traders also used the already-established route that later became part of the Oregon Trail as early Americans migrated west in search of their fortunes. Now those seeking nature can walk the same route that has been walked for hundreds of years. Soda Springs has many beautiful hiking trails where nature lovers can enjoy the natural beauty of the landscape ringed by majestic mountains and flanked by the stunning turquoise Bear Lake.
